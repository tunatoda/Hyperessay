“Masters, I pray you pray for me,
for I have deserved this death.”
-Mark Smeaton,
May 17, 1536


MUSICIAN FACES EXECUTION
FOR ADULTERY


Born c. 1512 - died 1536.

Death by execution— decapitation.

Mark Smeaton was a musician who served at the court of Henry VIII of England. He was suspected to have engaged in adultery with Queen Anne. After being tortured, Smeaton was pressured into giving a false, guilty confession that led to the deaths of 5 other men.

Out of the 5 other men accused, Smeaton was the only person to plead guilty— whether he did so because of the torture or the coaxing is unknown. As there was no image of Smeaton, the picture depicts a stringed instrument he would've played at his time.
