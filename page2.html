SIR RALEIGH BEHEADED FOR TREASON


Born c. 1552 - died 1618.

Death by execution— decapitation.

As one of the most notable figures of the Elizabethan Era (1558-1603), Sir Walter Raleigh was a soldier, writer, explorer, and a political figure. He faced trial for his suspected involvement with the Main Plot against Queen Elizabeth's successor, James I, and was charged with treason and the punishment of execution.

After being shown the axe that would be used to execute him shortly before his beheading, Raleigh spoke that "This is a sharp Medicine, but it is a Physician for all diseases and miseries."
